Image deconvolution has always been a hard inverse problem because of its mathematically ill-posed property. A recent work proposed deep image prior (DIP) which uses neural net structures to represent image prior information.

This work mainly focuses on the task of image deconvolution, using DIP to express image prior information and refine the domain of its objectives. proposes new energy functions for kernel-known and blind deconvolution respectively in terms of DIP, and uses ConvNet as the DIP structures to represent sharpness or other higher level priors of natural images, as well as the prior in degradation kernels. From the results on 6 standard test images, we first prove that DIP with ConvNet structure is strongly capable of capturing natural image prior in image deconvolution. Then we conclude that our DIP models significantly improve deconvolution performance compared with the baselines in both kernel-known and blind settings, in terms of either PSNR metric or deconvolution visual effects.
